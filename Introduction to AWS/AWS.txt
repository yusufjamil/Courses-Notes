AWS Introduction Notes (from the uploaded clip)
1. Traditional Infrastructure

In a traditional on-premises environment, companies purchase servers based on their expected peak usage.

Example
Normal traffic: 100 users
Peak traffic: 1000 users

To handle the peak, the company buys hardware capable of serving 1000 users.

Problem

For most of the day:

CPU remains idle
Memory remains unused
Storage is underutilized

This wastes money.

2. Overprovisioning

Definition

Overprovisioning means purchasing more computing resources than are normally required to prepare for future demand or traffic spikes.

Disadvantages
Expensive hardware
Wasted electricity
Higher maintenance costs
Resources stay idle most of the time

3. AWS Cloud Solution

AWS solves this problem using cloud computing.

Instead of buying servers:

Rent resources on demand.
Scale resources up when traffic increases.
Scale resources down when traffic decreases.

4. Pay-As-You-Go Model

The instructor mentions:

"With AWS you simply pay for what you need."

Meaning

You pay only for the resources you actually consume.

Examples:

EC2 running for 10 hours → Pay for 10 hours.
100 GB stored in S3 → Pay for 100 GB.
Lambda function executes 5000 times → Pay only for those executions.

5. Benefits
Lower upfront investment
No need to purchase physical servers
Elastic scaling
Reduced operational costs
Faster deployment
Better utilization of infrastructure

6. Key Interview Questions

What is overprovisioning?

Purchasing more infrastructure than is currently needed to prepare for future demand, resulting in idle resources and unnecessary cost.

How does AWS solve overprovisioning?

AWS allows customers to provision resources on demand and pay only for what they use, eliminating the need to maintain excess hardware.

What is the Pay-As-You-Go pricing model?

Customers are billed only for the AWS services and resources they actually consume instead of paying upfront for maximum capacity.

What is elasticity?

The ability to automatically increase or decrease resources based on workload.

What is scalability?

The ability to increase computing resources to handle increased workload.

Quick Revision
Traditional infrastructure = Buy servers first.
Overprovisioning = Buy more than needed.
Underutilization = Idle resources.
AWS = Rent resources.
Pay only for usage.
Scale up during peak demand.
Scale down when demand decreases.
